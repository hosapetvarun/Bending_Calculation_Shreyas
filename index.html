Engineering Formulas

Core equations used in the bending stress analysis based on Euler-Bernoulli beam theory.

Primary Formula
σ = (M × c) / I
  • • σ = Bending Stress (Pa or N/m²)
  • • M = Bending Moment (N·m)
  • • c = Distance to neutral axis (m)
  • • I = Moment of Inertia (m⁴)
Moment of Inertia
I = (b × h³) / 12
  • • b = Width of cross-section (m)
  • • h = Height/Thickness (m)
  • • For rectangular sections
  • • Neutral axis at centroid
Safety Factor
FOS = σ_yield / σ_max
  • • FOS > 1.5 (Typical minimum)
  • • σ_yield = Material yield stress
  • • σ_max = Maximum bending stress
  • • Al 6061: σ_yield = 240 MPa
